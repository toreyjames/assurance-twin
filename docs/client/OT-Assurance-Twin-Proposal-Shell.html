1. The Challenge: OT Asset Visibility Gap

Industrial organizations typically have only 50–80% visibility into their OT assets. Engineering documentation, OT security platforms, CMDB systems, and CMMS operate in silos, creating blind spots that compromise security, compliance, and operational efficiency.

Traditional tools produce lists—inventories of devices with attributes. But lists lack context: What does this device do? Where is it in the production process? Is it critical? How do we know it's correct? What's missing?

The OT Assurance Twin Difference

The Assurance Twin provides context-aware asset management—not just what you have, but what it does, where it is, how you know, and what's missing. It's an assurance layer that answers the hardest question: "Do we actually have what we think we have, can we prove it to regulators, and where are the gaps?"

2. Our Solution: OT Assurance Twin

2.1 Core Capabilities

  • Multi-Source Fusion: Ingests Engineering baseline, OT discovery, CMMS, Security findings, Network segmentation, and Incident data
  • Flexible Matching: 6 matching strategies (Tag ID, IP, Hostname, MAC, Fuzzy, Intelligent Pairing) with confidence scoring
  • Cross-Verification: Validates Engineering baseline against OT discovery reality—flags mismatches, unverified devices, suspicious classifications
  • Process-Aware Intelligence: Maps assets to process units and performs multi-layered completeness analysis (reference ranges, relative comparison, functional completeness, baseline tracking)
  • Explicit Gap Detection: Identifies blind spots (Engineering assets not on network) and orphans (OT assets not in baseline)
  • Multi-Source Enrichment: Links maintenance history, vulnerabilities, network zones, and incidents to canonical assets
  • Audit-Ready Outputs: Generates regulator-ready evidence with validation scores and cross-verification summaries

2.2 The 4-Phase Process

Phase 1: Ingest & Standardize

Accepts disparate CSV files from multiple sources. Auto-detects data source type from headers, normalizes field names across varied formats, merges multiple files of the same type, and deduplicates by tag_id/IP/hostname.

Phase 2: Match & Canonize

Matches engineering baseline to OT discovery using 6 strategies in order of confidence. Builds canonical asset records (one row per matched asset) and identifies gaps: blind spots and orphans.

Phase 3: Verify & Enrich

Cross-validates matches by checking field agreement and assigns confidence levels. Classifies devices by security tier. Enriches with multi-source data (CMMS work orders, CVEs, firewall zones, incidents). Maps to process units and performs multi-layered completeness analysis.

Phase 4: Analyze & Export

Calculates KPIs (coverage %, security coverage %, plant completeness scores). Generates AI recommendations (data quality issues, security gaps, unknown devices, OT collector deployment locations). Exports canonical inventory, blind spots, and orphans as CSV/JSON.

3.4 Strategic Add-On Options

Based on your priorities and organizational maturity, we offer strategic add-on options that build on the baseline to address specific business outcomes:

Option A: Regulatory & Assurance Focus (Always Available)

Estimated Additional LOE: 5–10 consulting days

What It Delivers: Transforms your baseline into regulator-ready evidence packs. Maps findings to NERC CIP, IEC 62443, FDA, or other relevant standards. Creates compliance-ready documentation with validation scores, traceability, and justification narratives that answer "how do we know" when regulators ask about asset completeness.

Why It Matters: Asset inventory accuracy is consistently cited as a top pain point in regulatory audits. Most organizations struggle to prove completeness when regulators ask. This option directly addresses that gap and positions you to confidently respond to audit inquiries.

Best For: Organizations subject to regulatory oversight (NERC CIP, IEC 62443, FDA, etc.) or preparing for upcoming audits. Universal concern in OT environments—even if not currently under audit, organizations need to be audit-ready.

Option B: OT Cyber Risk Reduction (Often Available)

Estimated Additional LOE: 5–10 consulting days

What It Delivers: Uses your canonical inventory to build a prioritized risk register. Identifies which blind spots and orphans pose the highest cyber risk. Creates remediation roadmap (90-day and 12-month views) with clear prioritization based on criticality, network exposure, and vulnerability data.

Why It Matters: CISOs and OT security teams are already investing heavily in OT security platforms (Claroty, Nozomi, Dragos), but struggle to prove coverage completeness and prioritize remediation. The Assurance Twin's blind spot detection and cross-verification directly addresses "what are we missing" and "where should we focus first."

Best For: Organizations with active OT security programs, CISO-driven initiatives, recent security incidents, or those trying to justify additional OT security investments.

Option C: Preventive Maintenance Optimization (Where Data Maturity Allows)

Estimated Additional LOE: 5–10 consulting days

What It Delivers: Uses your canonical inventory + CMMS data to identify under-maintained critical assets and over-maintained low-impact assets. Creates risk-based PM segmentation (Tier 1: must have robust PM, Tier 2: optimized intervals, Tier 3: candidates for PM reduction). Produces maintenance optimization playbook for your pilot plant.

Why It Matters: Predictive/preventive maintenance is a multi-billion dollar market with strong growth. Unplanned downtime costs manufacturing $50B+ annually. Many organizations have PM programs but lack data-driven prioritization. This option helps maintenance teams focus effort where it matters most.

Best For: Organizations with reasonably linked CMMS data (tag ↔ asset) and active maintenance/reliability programs. Best as follow-on after proving value with core pilot and Stage 1 options.

Option D: Turnaround Planning Intelligence (Timing-Dependent)

Estimated Additional LOE: 5–10 consulting days

What It Delivers: Uses your canonical inventory to validate and prioritize turnaround scope. Identifies high-risk blind spots that should be addressed during TA. Creates risk-based scope prioritization and deferral intelligence (what can safely be deferred vs. what's critical). Defines post-TA data capture plan to improve the canon.

Why It Matters: Turnarounds are massive, infrequent, high-stakes events. Better scope validation and prioritization can significantly reduce TA costs and risk. Organizations are willing to pay for intelligence that helps optimize these critical windows.

Best For: Organizations with a turnaround/shutdown planned within 12–18 months. Timing-dependent but high-value when applicable.

Option E: Digital Twin Readiness (Strategic)

Estimated Additional LOE: 3–5 consulting days

What It Delivers: Assesses data quality and completeness of your canonical inventory against digital twin requirements. Maps gaps (what's missing for DT foundation). Creates roadmap for what's needed (data, governance, integration) to progress from Assurance Twin to full digital twin.

Why It Matters: Digital twins are a strategic priority for many organizations, but lack of clean, complete asset data is consistently cited as the #1 barrier. The Assurance Twin provides that foundation, and this option explicitly maps the path forward.

Best For: Organizations with active or planned digital twin initiatives. Best positioned as strategic narrative layer on top of baseline + assurance.
